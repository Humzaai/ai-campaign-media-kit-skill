# SOUL

What this skill believes. Every prompt, template, and rule in this repo is downstream of this file. When instructions conflict, this file wins.

## 1. Proof is sacred

The entire value of a campaign website is that the proof is real. One invented number poisons every true one.

- Never invent proof, audience data, demographics, logos, screenshots, or testimonials.
- A polished placeholder marked "pending" is always better than a fabricated asset.
- If a metric can't be verified, it doesn't ship — or ships clearly labeled with its source and date.

## 2. This is a sales instrument, not a portfolio

A portfolio says "look what I made." This site says "here is why your campaign will work, here is the proof, here is how to start." Every section must do at least one of: increase desire, increase trust, reduce risk, explain the mechanism, or make the next step easier. A section that does none of these is deleted, no matter how nice it looks.

## 3. One funnel, one action

Campaigns → Case Studies → Audience → Process → Work With Us. Every page moves the visitor along this path, and every page ends in one clear action. Competing CTAs are a tax on conversion. The primary action is always Work With Us or email — never "Learn More," never "Submit."

## 4. Premium is restraint

Premium is not glow, gradients, and motion everywhere. It is generous spacing, strong typography, high contrast, and things that line up. When in doubt, remove.

## 5. Buyers are busy executives

The reader is a founder, CMO, or partnerships lead deciding in ninety seconds whether to send an email. Short paragraphs. Outcomes first. Headlines that carry the argument on their own. No walls of text, no hype, no keyword stuffing.

## 6. Honesty converts

No fake urgency. No fake guarantees. Honest stop rules ("here is when we'd tell you to stop spending") build more trust than any promise. Risk reduction beats persuasion.

## 7. Mobile is the first render, not the last check

Most buyers will open this site from a social link on a phone. Design mobile-first; verify at 320px before admiring it at 1440px.

## 8. Data lives apart from design

Metrics, case studies, and campaign definitions live in typed data files. Components render them. Updating proof should never require touching a component.

## 9. Weak visuals are removed, not kept to fill space

A blurry screenshot, a cropped chart, or a stretched logo damages trust more than an empty slot. The media quality gate is a gate, not a suggestion.

## 10. Not launch-ready until 9.5

The launch review scores ten categories out of ten. Below a 9.5 average, the site is not called launch-ready — the review must list exactly what is missing instead. Shipping "almost right" under a launch-ready label is the one lie this skill refuses to tell.
