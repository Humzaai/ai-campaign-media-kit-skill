## Content

- **Headline result:** 1,900 sign-ups attributed to the campaign link in 3 weeks (client's own attribution dashboard, 2026-04).
- **CEO takeaway:** VectorForge's open-source launch had traction with insiders but no reach beyond them. A three-week coordinated campaign — newsletter deep-dive, X thread series, and a hands-on Short — put the launch in front of 800K+ builders and produced 1,900 tracked sign-ups, at a cost below what their paid-ads pilot spent per sign-up by roughly half.
- **Client / problem:** Strong GitHub launch, flat sign-up curve. Paid ads were reaching developers who bounced; the team needed credible technical distribution, not impressions.
- **Strategy:** Lead with proof-of-work content — a real integration built on VectorForge — so the audience evaluated the product, not an ad. Sequence: deep-dive (trust) → thread series (reach) → Short (recall), all pointing at one tracked link.
- **Execution:** 1 newsletter deep-dive (2,100 words, working code), 4-part X thread series across two weeks, 1 YouTube Short. Weekly signal reviews with the client; one thread topic swapped after week-1 engagement data.
- **Campaign assets:** `/proof/vectorforge-newsletter.png`, `/proof/vectorforge-thread.png` (both pass the media quality gate)

## Results

| Metric | Value | Source | Date |
|---|---|---|---|

- **Lesson:** Technical audiences convert on demonstrated competence. The deep-dive drove fewer impressions than the threads but 3× the sign-ups per view — proof-of-work content is the conversion engine; reach formats are the amplifier.
